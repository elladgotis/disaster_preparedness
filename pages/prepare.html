Prepare Now

Being prepared can save lives and reduce the impact of disasters. In the Philippines, where typhoons, earthquakes, and floods are common, it's crucial to take steps now — not later.

📝 1. Make a Family Emergency Plan

  • 📍 Identify safe areas in your home and community.
  • 📞 Assign emergency contacts (local and out-of-town).
  • 📅 Plan meeting points if separated.
  • 👪 Teach children what to do during emergencies.

🎒 2. Build a Go-Bag (Emergency Kit)

  • ✅ Bottled water (3-day supply)
  • ✅ Canned or ready-to-eat food
  • ✅ Flashlight & batteries
  • ✅ First aid kit and medicines
  • ✅ Whistle and multi-tool
  • ✅ Clothes, masks, and hygiene items
  • ✅ Copies of important documents
  • ✅ Power bank and charger

📢 3. Stay Informed

  • 📱 Follow official sources like PAGASA, PHIVOLCS, NDRRMC.
  • 🔊 Listen to local authorities and evacuation orders.
  • 🗺️ Know evacuation routes and centers in your area.

Tip: Practice your emergency plan regularly — especially before the rainy season.
