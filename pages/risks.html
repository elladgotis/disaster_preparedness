Know the Risks (Philippines)

The Philippines faces a wide range of natural and man-made hazards due to its location along the Pacific Ring of Fire and in the path of tropical cyclones. Understanding these risks is essential for your safety and preparedness.

Common Disasters in the Philippines:

  • 🌀 Typhoons – Around 20 tropical cyclones annually; 5 are destructive.
  • 🌊 Flooding – Caused by heavy rain, poor drainage, or storm surges.
  • 🌋 Volcanic Eruptions – Over 20 active volcanoes like Mayon and Taal.
  • 🌍 Earthquakes – Frequent and powerful due to tectonic activity.
  • 🌊 Tsunamis – Triggered by undersea earthquakes or eruptions.
  • 🔥 Fires – Common in dense urban areas during dry season.
  • 🚨 Landslides – Caused by heavy rain or earthquakes.
  • ☣️ Man-made Hazards – Industrial or transport-related incidents.

Action Step: Identify which of these risks are most likely in your area. Check PHIVOLCS, PAGASA, or NDRRMC websites for hazard maps and alerts.
